FEDERAL AND STATE AGENCIES

  •   NSF - CMMI#1761009: REU Supplement: Optimizing Trauma Care Network Design

    This REU Supplement with allow Dr. Parikh to recruit two undergraduate ISE students and provide them with vital research experience in using data analytics and optimization methods in trauma care. The students will help cleanse and organize data we have received from the States of OH and IN, analyze them using appropriate statistical tools, and help the current graduate students in model validation and evaluation studies, and GIS tool development.

  •   NSF - CMMI#1761009 and CMMI#1761022: Collaborative Research: Optimizing Trauma Care Network Design (2018-21)

    Trauma is a leading cause of death in younger populations. The trauma care network should ensure that severely injured patients receive the right care in the right amount of time, but maldistribution of trauma care facilities can result in under-treatment of severely injured patients as well as expensive over-treatment of less-severely injured patients. This project will facilitate coordinated network design between state agencies and hospital networks, two key stakeholders in the trauma care system. Innovative optimization methods to coordinate the bilevel decision making process for trauma center location will be developed. This will enable trauma policy makers to quantitatively benchmark trauma care regionally and nationally in terms of triage errors and costs. Our collaborators include practicing trauma surgeons from Johns Hopkins University and WSU, EMS chief, and EMS/Trauma Divisions at the State of OH and IN.

  •   NSF - CMMI#1548394 and CMMI#1548404: Collaborative Research: EAGER: A New Science of Visual Experience (2015-18)

    The multidisciplinary research team consisting of academics (engineering, psychology, and computer science) and two industry personnel proposes moving visual experience of a three dimensional environment from the realm of intuition and experience to analytical science. The specific focus of this grant will be on developing a general set of analytical models that emerge from the analysis of a variety of context-specific human-environment interactions (e.g., nurse in CCU, shopper in a retail store). With a solid grounding in the basic psychology of Perception-Action, the analytical models will integrate three-dimensional spatial relationships with the human eye’s field of vision and the physical attributes of a human. Most significantly, the research will consider complex dynamics resulting from human movement in the space, with all the attendant changes in visual angles, and the appearance and disappearance of visual obstacles. Human performance will be empirically examined in a Virtual Environment in order to validate the proposed analytical metrics of visual experience. Project website: retail.

  •   NSF - CMMI#1405265 and CMMI#1405357: Collaborative Research: GOALI: Optimal Inpatient Discharge Planning (2014-18)

    Through this research grant, our academic-industry team intends to offer insights into improving patient flow through inpatient units and efficiently managing staff workload. We propose to study the inpatient discharge planning problem, i.e., optimally organizing and managing discharge processes to minimize inpatient admission and discharge delay. We provide an approach to tradeoff competing issues of upstream patient boarding, inpatient discharge lateness, and inpatient unit workload integration. We incorporate various sources of uncertainties arising in discharge service processing time, wait time between consecutive services, and arrival time of inpatient bed request. Our approach will serve as a benchmark for existing ad hoc rules while developing new near-optimal and implementable discharge policies. This research will allow hospital managers to increase patient satisfaction and service throughput, policy makers to develop improved benchmarks for hospital workflows, and front-line providers to make intelligent data-driven decisions. Project website: idp.

  •   NSF - CMMI#1235061 and CMMI#1235283: Collaborative Research: GOALI: Warehouse-Inventory-Transportation Problem for Supply Chains (2012-15)

    Our academic-industry team proposes to develop a set of innovative models and solutions for analyzing the three-way interaction among warehousing, inventory, and transportation decisions. For this purpose, we introduce for the first time in academic literature the warehouse-inventory-transportation problem (WITP) for multi-echelon and multi-period supply chains. Decisions associated with products with differing life-cycles flowing through the supply chain are also considered in the WITP. This project is very unique as it will develop managerial insights on the the impact of tactical and operational warehousing decisions in generating supply chain plans. Project website: witp.

  •   Ohio Dept of Public Safety:  Trauma Cost vs. Care: Estimations for Ohio’s Current Trauma System and Evaluation of Alternative Configurations (2019-20)

    This study focuses on estimating the cost of ‘prehospital’ and ‘inpatient’ care for the current trauma system in OH, estimating trauma care corresponding to these costs, characterizing a given trauma system across a various cost-care measures, and evaluating comparative effectiveness of alternative trauma system configurations in terms of cost and care.

  •   Ohio Dept of Public Safety: Allocation of trauma resources in the State of Ohio: A data driven approach for current performance assessment and benchmarking (2018-20)

    This study focuses on identifying the resources at various EMS agencies in the state, documenting their process improvement activities, and benchmarking them against acceptable metrics via data analytics approaches.

  •   Ohio Dept of Public Safety: Refining the Definitions of Under- and Over-Triage: A Data Analytics and Consensus-Based Approach (2017-19)

    This study focuses on refining the existing definitions in academic literature about UT and OT, associating them with factors that cause them, and building a consensus with trauma professionals on the appropriate use of these definitions in the content of patient safety and trauma care cost.

  • Ohio Dept of Public Safety: Impact of Inter-Facility Transfers and Secondary Triage on Trauma Care in the State of Ohio (2015-17)

    This study -- renewal of a previous study -- will identify the burden of inter-facility transfer of trauma patients in the state of Ohio and provide insights into how the overall care access and quality experienced by a trauma patient can be improved.

  •  Ohio Dept of Public Safety: Spectrum of Trauma Care in the State of Ohio - Assessment and Improvement of Quality of Care (2014-15)

    This pilot study is geared towards understanding the current state of trauma care in Ohio. We will analyze data provided by the ODPS to develop insights into reasons for suboptimal care to trauma patients.

  •  New England - Veterans Engineering Resource Center (NE-VERC): Predicting Discharge Disposition of Newly-Admitted Veterans at a VA Medical Center (2014-16)

    In line with the Utilization Management project at the VHA, the goal of this 2-year project is to develop a model that can help physicians and other care providers better predict the disposition of a newly-admitted patient at Boston VA medical center.

  •  Dayton VA: Systems and Human Factors Engineering Analysis of the E-Consult Program Innovation (2012-14)

    The VA initiated electronic consults (e-consults) to provide specialty consults to veterans throughout the U.S. The overall goals were to improve access, communication and coordination of care for veterans to health care providers. Intended outcomes of e-consults are to increase access to specialty care in rural and underserved settings, reduce wait times, reduce fee and travel costs, improve Veteran and staff satisfaction, increase provider knowledge, competencies and professional training hours, and maintain or improve patient safety. This study documented the diabetes, GI, and neurosurgery e-consult process by using chart reviews, job shadowing, and interviews, and statistical and simulation modeling, to identify potential risks and barriers during each step in the e-consult process.

  •  Dayton VA: Surgical Case Scheduling

    This project involved developing an optimization model and an algorithm (based on dual bin-packing concept) to generate near-optimal surgical case schedules accounting for several real-world constraints. These include availability of operating room, physician, nurses, and specialized equipment, patient priorities (condition, start times), and other operational conditions.
